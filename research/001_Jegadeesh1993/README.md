# Research 001 - Momentum Investing

## Paper

**Title**

Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers:
Implications for Stock Market Efficiency

**Authors**

Narasimhan Jegadeesh
Sheridan Titman

**Year**

1993

---

## Background

Why was this research conducted?

At that time, the Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH) suggested that past prices should not contain predictive information about future returns. Most return predictability studies focused on long-term reversals, while short- to medium-term continuation had not been systematically examined.

---

## Research Question

Can past intermediate-term returns predict future intermediate-term returns? Dose the momentum exist? And what's the reason behind its existence?

---

## Hypothesis

Stocks with strong past performance will continue to outperform in the near future because the market does not fully incorporate information immediately.

---

## Methodology

The key here is to control variables to test which exactly term contribute the majority of the profit. It decomposites the profit into 3 parts: 
1.Expected Return Dispersion
2.Common Factor Persistence
3.Firm-specific Persistence

The first two terms are the normal behaviours that the market have: if you face more risk you will naturally have more return. But the third term would reflect market incompleness since the information isn't fully reflectly in the prices. The paper used the method of control of variables to prove that if we use this momentum strategy, the profit is mainly contributed by the third term which means that the momentum is exploiting the market misfunction of not fully digesting the information.

Futher, once the paper proves it's the third term that contributes the profit, it keeps doing robust tests including testing the strategy in different size groups, beta groups, time groups to make sure this is a truly valid conclusion. And finally it directly tests whether the hypothesis is correct: we suppose it's the market cannot instantly reflect the information then we test the hypothesis at the time where firms' public annoucements are made. And in the end we analyze the results and make the conclusion.
